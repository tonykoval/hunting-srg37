Appendix B Bibliography

Sources are grouped by role. The three classification papers in $\S$B.2 are the authority for the number $6802$.

B.1   Foundations

  • R. E. A. C. Paley. On orthogonal matrices. J. Math. Phys. 12 (1933), 311–320.
  • R. C. Bose. Strongly regular graphs, partial geometries and partially balanced designs. Pacific J. Math. 13 (1963), 389–419.
  • A. E. Brouwer, A. M. Cohen, A. Neumaier. Distance-Regular Graphs. Springer, 1989. (Krein conditions, absolute bound.)
  • A. E. Brouwer, H. Van Maldeghem. Strongly Regular Graphs. Encyclopedia of Mathematics and its Applications 182, Cambridge University Press, 2022.
  • A. E. Brouwer. Parameters of strongly regular graphs (online table). aeb.win.tue.nl/graphs/srg/srgtab.html.
  • J. J. Seidel. A survey of two-graphs. In: Colloquio Internazionale sulle Teorie Combinatorie, Roma 1973, Vol. I, Accad. Naz. Lincei (1976), 481–511.

B.2   The $(37,18,8,9)$ classification

  • E. Spence. Strongly regular graphs (data collection, incl. the $\mathrm{SRG}(37,18,8,9)$ file sr371889). maths.gla.ac.uk/~es/srgraphs.php. (6760 graphs.)
  • B. D. McKay, E. Spence. Classification of regular two-graphs on 36 and 38 vertices. Australas. J. Combin. 24 (2001), 293–300. (The "million unsearched neighbourhoods" source.)
  • D. Crnković, M. Maksimović. Strongly regular graphs with parameters (37,18,8,9) having nontrivial automorphisms. Art Discrete Appl. Math. 3 (2020) #P2.10. (+6 graphs.)
  • M. Maksimović, S. Rukavina. New regular two-graphs on 38 and 42 vertices. Math. Commun. 27 (2022), 151–161. (+36 graphs; total 6802.)

B.3   Methods & tools

  • C. D. Godsil, B. D. McKay. Constructing cospectral graphs. Aequationes Math. 25 (1982), 257–268. (Godsil–McKay switching, Ch 5.)
  • B. D. McKay, A. Piperno. Practical graph isomorphism, II. J. Symbolic Comput. 60 (2014), 94–112. (nauty, canonical forms.)
  • M. Kirchweger, S. Szeider. SAT modulo symmetries for graph generation. Proc. CP 2021, LIPIcs 210. (SMS, Ch 4.)
  • M. Kirchweger, S. Szeider. Smart Cubing for Graph Search: A Comparative Study. arXiv:2501.17201 (2025). (Ch 4, §4.3.)
  • The CryptoMiniSat, CaDiCaL and kissat SAT solvers (M. Soos; A. Biere et al.). (Ch 4 back ends.)
  • de Caen-style linear $B$-block CEGAR — project method; implementation in the companion repository orbit-gen (src/de_caen.rs), reproducing thesis counts for $\mathrm{SRG}(25,12,5,6)$. (Ch 4, §4.2.)

B.4   AI-assisted construction

  • F. Charton, J. S. Ellenberg, A. Z. Wagner, G. Williamson. PatternBoost: Constructions in Mathematics with a Little Help from AI. arXiv:2411.00566 (2024). (Transformer warm-start, Ch 6, §6.6.)
  • AlphaEvolve — evolutionary code generation with LLMs (Google DeepMind, 2025). (Ch 7, framework 10.)
  • cpro1 / multi-agent debate / MCTS-policy — project frameworks; implementations in orbit-gen/scripts/llm_*.py. (Ch 7.)
Citation hygiene

During this project, LLM assistants produced several fabricated or stale citations (a non-existent "Smith–Yang", a misattributed inline reference, a hallucinated date). Every entry above was checked against the arXiv abstract or journal page. Project-internal method names (de Caen-style CEGAR, cpro1) are labelled as such rather than dressed in a false bibliographic citation.
