Appendix A Notation & Glossary

A.1   Symbols

SymbolMeaning
$(n, k, \lambda, \mu)$SRG parameters: order, degree, edge-common-neighbours, non-edge-common-neighbours
$A$Adjacency matrix ($0/1$, symmetric, zero diagonal)
$I,\ J$Identity matrix; all-ones matrix
$r,\ s$The two non-principal eigenvalues, $r > s$; for the target $\tfrac{-1\pm\sqrt{37}}{2}$
$f,\ g$Multiplicities of $r,\ s$; for the target $f = g = 18$
$N(v)$Neighbourhood of $v$; for the target a $(18,8)$-regular graph
$c_{ij}$Number of common neighbours of $i$ and $j$
$\mathrm{viol}(\Gamma)$Total deviation of $c_{ij}$ from its target ($8$ on edges, $9$ on non-edges)
$|\mathrm{Aut}\,\Gamma|$Order of the automorphism group
$P(q)$Paley graph on $\mathbb{F}_q$, $q \equiv 1 \pmod 4$
$\omega$Clique number (Hoffman/Delsarte bound $\omega \le 1 - k/s$)

A.2   Glossary

Strongly regular graph (SRG). A $k$-regular graph in which adjacent vertices share $\lambda$ and non-adjacent vertices share $\mu$ common neighbours.

Conference graph. An SRG with $(n, \tfrac{n-1}{2}, \tfrac{n-5}{4}, \tfrac{n-1}{4})$, $n \equiv 1 \pmod 4$; eigenvalues are irrational and multiplicities equal. The target $(37,18,8,9)$ is the smallest unclassified one.

Paley graph. Vertices $\mathbb{F}_q$; $u \sim v$ iff $u - v$ is a nonzero square. $P(37)$ is our reference $\mathrm{SRG}(37,18,8,9)$.

Regular two-graph. A switching class of graphs encoding an SRG up to Seidel switching; the objects McKay–Spence classify on $36$ and $38$ vertices.

Godsil–McKay switching. A local operation on a cell $C$ where every outside vertex sees $0$, half, or all of $C$; produces a cospectral graph. Seidel switching is the $|C|=2$ case.

graph6 (g6). McKay's compact ASCII encoding of a graph, used for canonical-form comparison.

Canonical form. A unique representative of an isomorphism class, computed by nauty; two graphs are isomorphic iff their canonical forms are identical.

CEGAR. Counterexample-guided abstraction refinement: solve a relaxed model, find a violated constraint, add it, repeat.

SMS / CCL. SAT modulo symmetries; complete canonicity check. A propagator adds, during search, the clause "this partial graph is not lexicographically minimal in its isomorphism class", pruning isomorphic branches dynamically.

IPASIR-UP. The solver interface that lets an external propagator (here, C-nauty) inject clauses mid-search — the mechanism behind SMS+CCL.

Cube-and-conquer. Split a hard SAT instance into many "cubes" (partial assignments) solved in parallel; effective when a few cubes are hard and most are trivial.

Partial difference set (PDS). A subset $D$ of a group $G$ giving a Cayley graph that is strongly regular; an algebraic route to SRG construction.

Basin-hopping. Perturb a local optimum, re-optimise, and keep the better basin — a global-search wrapper around local search.

$\#6803$. Shorthand for the goal: a valid $\mathrm{SRG}(37,18,8,9)$ isomorphic to none of the $6802$ catalog graphs.
