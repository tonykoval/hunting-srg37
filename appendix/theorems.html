Appendix C Theorems & Proofs

The mathematical spine of the hunt, collected for reference. Each result is stated for $\mathrm{SRG}(37,18,8,9)$ specifically where that sharpens it; the chapter in which it is used is noted.

C.1   Parameters and rigidity

Theorem C.1 (regularity is forced) — Ch 4

A graph on 37 vertices in which every edge lies in exactly $\lambda=8$ triangles and every non-edge in exactly $\mu=9$ common-neighbour pairs is automatically $18$-regular, hence a genuine SRG(37,18,8,9).

Proof. For adjacent $u,v$, counting edges between $N(u)\setminus N[v]$ and $N(v)\setminus N[u]$ gives $(\mu-1)(\deg u-\lambda-1)=(\mu-1)(\deg v-\lambda-1)$; since $\mu=9>1$, $\deg u=\deg v$, and connectivity propagates equal degree everywhere. The standard identity $(n-k-1)\mu=k(k-\lambda-1)$ then forces $k=18$. $\square$   (This is why the SAT encoding omits the ${\sim}10^{17}$ degree clauses.)

Theorem C.2 (any #6803 is rigid) — Ch 3

There are exactly 40 graphs $\mathrm{SRG}(37,18,8,9)$ with a nontrivial automorphism, so $6802 = 40_{\text{closed}} + 6762_{\text{open}}$, and a $6803$rd graph (if any) has $|\mathrm{Aut}|=1$.

Proof / provenance. Behbahani–Lam reduce a prime-order automorphism to an orbit-matrix prototype system; the fixed-point bound $\mathrm{fix}\le\frac{\max(\lambda,\mu)}{k-r}v\approx 21$ leaves only small primes, whose prototype systems Crnković–Maksimović (2020) solve exhaustively, obtaining exactly 40. Maksimović–Rukavina (2022) lift these to the catalog count $6802$ as a stated lower bound. $\square$

C.2   The two-graph correspondence

Theorem C.3 (Taylor 1977) — Ch 3½

A two-graph on $n$ points is regular iff each of its $n$ descendants is strongly regular with $\mu=k/2$. For $n=38$ the descendants are exactly the conference graphs SRG(37,18,8,9), and the regular two-graph is a symmetric conference matrix $C$ of order 38 with $C^2=37I$.

Proof idea. The Seidel matrix $S=J-I-2A$ of any graph in the switching class has spectrum determined by the two-graph; regularity $\Leftrightarrow$ $S$ has the two eigenvalues $\pm\sqrt{n-1}$, i.e. $S^2=(n-1)I$. Descending at $v$ (switch $v$ isolated, delete it) yields adjacency $a_{ij}=[\{i,j,v\}\in\Delta]$, an SRG$(n-1,k,\lambda,k/2)$. $\square$

Theorem C.4 (de Caen's identity, 1998) — Ch 4

With $A_1$ ($\lambda$-regular on $N(v)$) and $A_2$ ($(k{-}\mu)$-regular on $V\setminus N[v]$), $\chi_{A_2}(x)=(x-(k-\mu))\,((x-r)(x-s))^{f-k}\, \chi_{A_1}(-(x+\mu-\lambda))/(x+\mu)$. For $(37,18,8,9)$, $k=f=18$ gives $\chi_{A_2}(x)=(x-9)\,\chi_{A_1}(-(x+1))/(x+9)$, the division exact.

Reference. D. de Caen, Eur. J. Combin. 19 (1998) 559–565. The orbit-gen implementation reproduces it symbolically and is unit-tested against Paley(13/29) and $T(9)$. $\square$

C.3   Prescribed symmetry and the towers

Lemma C.5 ($p\cdot f\le 37$) — Ch 3½

A regular two-graph on 38 with an automorphism of prime order $p$ fixing $f$ points satisfies $p\cdot f\le 37$.

Proof sketch. $\sigma$ commutes with $S$ ($S^2=37I$); the compression $M_0$ of $S$ to the $\sigma$-invariant subspace satisfies $M_0^2=37I$. The diagonal of this identity on the fixed block reads $p\cdot f+(\text{nonneg sums of squares})=37$. $\square$   (Consequence: the only fixed-point-free cyclic cases are $p=2,f=0$ — impossible — and $p=19,f=0$ — done; every $f>0$ cyclic case has a symmetric descendant and is already in the 40.)

Theorem C.6 (seven towers) — Ch 4

Under the gauge $e[0,1]=0$ and the static row-lex chain, every cube outside the seven towers $\{0,128,192,224,240,248,252\}$ is empty; a $6803$rd graph lies in exactly one tower.

Proof (computational). Three split rounds: cube size 5 ($28/32$ UNSAT), size 8 ($249/256$ UNSAT, residual the staircase $1^k0^{8-k}$), size 11 ($49/56$ UNSAT). Each "empty" is a sound EXHAUSTED certificate from CaDiCaL $+$ the nauty canonicity propagator; every finer split leaves exactly one hard child per tower, so the residual never drops below seven. $\square$

Theorem C.7 (soundness; CANON_CCL caveat) — Ch 12

(i) The default row-lex $+$ nauty canonicity loses no isomorphism class: over a full cube partition it enumerates the same set of two-graph orbits as a redundant search (verified to exhaustion, $N=10,14$). (ii) Column-major CANON_CCL is lossless over a full partition but unsound combined with the seven-tower restriction — its column-minimal representative can fall into a row-lex-pruned cube, a false UNSAT. The live run stays on the sound canonicity. $\square$
