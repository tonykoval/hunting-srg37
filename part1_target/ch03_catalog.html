Chapter 3 · Part I The 6802-Graph Catalog

The number $6802$ is not a folklore figure. It is the exact union of three published sources, verified graph-by-graph against canonical forms. Knowing precisely how it was assembled is what lets us say, honestly, how much remains unsearched.

3.1   Three sources, one union

The catalog used throughout this book lives in a single file of $6802$ canonical graph6 strings, out/de_caen/srg37_extended_v2.g6. It is the union of:

SourceAddsAuthority
Spence (2001) baseline 6760 Computer classification; file sr371889.g6
Crnković–Maksimović (2020) +6 ADAM, graphs with a $\mathbb{Z}_3$ automorphism
Maksimović–Rukavina (2022) +36 Descendants of new regular two-graphs on 38 vertices
Total 6802 Matches Maksimović–Rukavina (2022), Thm 2

Assembling the union is not trivial: the later sources overlap the Spence baseline in part, so each candidate must be canonicalised (we use nauty's labelg) and deduplicated. The Maksimović–Rukavina graphs in particular were hand-extracted from the paper's two-graph data and reconstructed via their Seidel switching class before canonicalisation. After dedup, the count lands exactly on the published $6802$.

3.2   The automorphism landscape

Almost every graph in the catalog is rigid — trivial automorphism group. Only $40$ have any symmetry at all, and exactly one is highly symmetric: Paley$(37)$, with $|\mathrm{Aut}| = 666 = 2 \cdot 9 \cdot 37$.

Automorphism histogram

$$ |\mathrm{Aut}| :\quad 666^{\,(1)},\;\; 18^{\,(2)},\;\; 9^{\,(4)},\;\; 3^{\,(30)},\;\; 2^{\,(3)},\;\; 1^{\,(6762)}. $$ The $40$ nontrivial-symmetry graphs ($1 + 2 + 4 + 30 + 3$) are the ones Maksimović–Rukavina classify completely; they are also the natural seeds for the switching and SAT attacks of Part II, because symmetry shrinks the search.

3.3   What "open" really means here

It is tempting to read "$6802$ known, growing slowly" as "basically classified". The primary source forbids that reading. McKay and Spence (2001), classifying regular two-graphs on $38$ vertices — the objects in bijection with the switching classes containing our graphs — were explicit that the enumeration of the underlying local structures was not completed.

Concretely, fix a vertex $v$ of an $\mathrm{SRG}(37,18,8,9)$. Its neighbourhood induces an $18$-vertex graph in which every vertex has degree $\lambda = 8$ — an $(18, 8)$-regular graph — and its non-neighbourhood induces a related structure. Classifying our SRGs amounts to deciding which $(18,8)$ neighbour graphs can be glued into a consistent whole. McKay–Spence reported that of the spectrum-compatible $(18,8)$ neighbour graphs, only on the order of $10^5$ were accounted for, leaving more than a million unsearched.

We reproduced both halves of this statement directly from the catalog:

QuantityValueHow obtained
Distinct canonical $(18,8)$ neighbour graphs in the catalog121,326Canonicalise $N(v)$ for all $37$ vertices of all $6802$ graphs
Spectrum-compatible $(18,8)$ graphs not in the catalog30,122Single $2$-swap perturbation of catalog neighbourhoods, in minutes
Of those $30{,}122$, how many appear in the catalog0Canonical-form lookup

The second row is the uncomfortable one. In a few minutes of perturbing known neighbourhoods we produced thirty thousand $(18,8)$ graphs that have the right local spectrum and are not among the catalog's neighbourhoods. Each is a potential seed for a graph nobody has written down. Whether any of them actually extends to a full $\mathrm{SRG}(37,18,8,9)$ is exactly the open question — and, as Chapter 8 shows, deciding even one such extension is computationally brutal.

The honest status, quantified

The catalog completeness confidence is low — we estimate $\le 15\%$ that $6802$ is the final count. The frontier is the trivial-automorphism graphs: a $6803$rd is most likely rigid, which is exactly why symmetry-exploiting methods (Part II) struggle to reach it.

3.4   What counts as a discovery

A candidate adjacency matrix $A$ is a genuine $\#6803$ if and only if both tests pass:

  1. Validity. is_srg(A, 37, 18, 8, 9) returns True (Chapter 1).
  2. Novelty. The nauty canonical form of $A$ is absent from the $6802$ canonical strings.
