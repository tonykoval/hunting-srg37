Hunting SRG(37)

The Search for a 6803rd Strongly Regular Graph on (37, 18, 8, 9)

Tony Koval

Content © 2026 CC-BY-4.0  ·  Code MIT  ·  Version 1.0

AbstractFront Matter

The strongly regular graph parameters $(37, 18, 8, 9)$ form the smallest conference-graph case that has never been completely classified. A graph exists — the Paley graph $P(37)$ — and the published lower bound on the number of such graphs up to isomorphism is $6802$, the union of Spence (2001), Crnković–Maksimović (2020) and Maksimović–Rukavina (2022). But McKay and Spence (2001) left over a million spectrum-compatible neighbour graphs unsearched, so completeness of the list is open.

This book documents twelve attacks, across five families, on the question of whether a $6803$rd graph exists: SAT and constraint solving (CEGAR over symmetric seeds, Smart Cubing, SMS+CCL cube-and-conquer, neighbour extension); Godsil–McKay switching; local and continuous optimisation (basin-hopping, simulated annealing, Euclidean embedding); five large-language-model frameworks; and a purpose-built backtracking extender. Every attack returned zero new graphs.

The result is a precise empirical map of the wall: where each method stops, why it stops, and what a serious assault — distributed canonicity-propagated cube-and-conquer, a compiled bitset extender, or a completeness proof — would require. The online edition is interactive (a feasibility calculator, a Paley$(37)$ viewer, an SRG verifier, a live local-search demo, a filterable scoreboard); the print edition is a linear monograph.
